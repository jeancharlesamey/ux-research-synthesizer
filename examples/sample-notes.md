# Sample Interview Notes

Use these example notes to test the UX Research Synthesizer.

## Example 1: Fintech App Study (Extended)

**Interview 1 — Jamie, small business owner**

"I check my accounts multiple times a day, but the mobile app is slow to load. Takes like 3-4 seconds every time. I've given up and just use the website on my phone. It's really frustrating because I want to use mobile for convenience, but it defeats the purpose when it's so slow. I've actually switched to checking my bank's app instead, which loads instantly. The transactions view is clean when it finally loads, but that delay is a killer. My customers sometimes pay me on the go and I want to verify deposits immediately, but this app doesn't let me do that efficiently."

**Interview 2 — Alex, freelancer**

"I love the transactions view, but I can't filter by date range. I have to scroll through months of data. On a working day, I might do 20 transactions. When tax time comes around, I need to find all transactions from a specific month and it's incredibly tedious. I end up exporting everything to a spreadsheet just to filter properly. It would be so easy if there was a date picker. Also, I wish I could search by vendor name or notes. Sometimes I forget exactly what a transaction was for and have to dig through my email to find context."

**Interview 3 — Morgan, accountant**

"The CSV export is helpful, but the date format changes between exports. Sometimes it's MM/DD/YYYY, sometimes DD/MM/YYYY. Makes reconciliation a nightmare. When I'm reconciling client accounts with their statements, inconsistent formatting causes errors. I have to manually fix it in Excel before I can match transactions. It's a waste of time and introduces risk of mistakes. Also, the export doesn't include notes or categories, so I lose context. I've asked support about this and they said it's a known limitation. For a fintech app in 2024, this feels broken."

**Interview 4 — Sam, accountant**

"The interface is intuitive and I appreciate the clean design. The spending insights are actually useful—I can see trends by category. What I need is better reconciliation tools though. I spend a lot of time manually verifying transactions. A bulk action feature would help—like being able to mark multiple transactions as reconciled at once. Right now I have to click each one individually, which is tedious for high-volume accounts."

## Example 2: SaaS Analytics Dashboard

**Interview 1 — Elena, product manager**

"The dashboard loads but I can't customize what metrics I see. I have to scroll past things I don't care about. A lot of wasted time."

**Interview 2 — Dev, data analyst**

"I need to download reports weekly. Right now I screenshot the dashboard and paste into Google Sheets. Takes 30 minutes every time."

**Interview 3 — Priya, operations manager**

"The real-time data is great, but alerts don't work right. I've missed critical issues because I didn't see the email notification. Happened twice last month."
